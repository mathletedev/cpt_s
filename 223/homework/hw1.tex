\section*{Problem 1}

Let $N(k)$ be the number of nodes of a complete binary tree at level $k$. \\
Hypothesis: $N(k) = 2^k$. \\
Base case: If $k = 0$, then $N(0) = 1$. \\
Inductive case: Each node has exactly two children
\begin{equation}
  \begin{split}
    N(k) & = 2N(k - 1) \\
    & = 2 \cdot 2N(k - 2) \\
    & = 2^mN(k - m) \\
    & = 2^kN(k - k) \\
    & = 2^kN(0) \\
    & = 2^k \cdot 1 \\
    & = 2^k
  \end{split}
\end{equation}
Therefore, $N(k) = 2^k$.
